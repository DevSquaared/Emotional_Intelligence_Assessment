Section A · The Nova Project — one case study, five questions

The Nova Project — Case Study

Kabir — newly promoted team lead Riya — senior analyst, struggling Arjun — vocal high-performer Priya & Dev — junior members in conflict You — experienced team member

Your organisation has assigned a cross-functional team to deliver "Project Nova" — a high-priority client proposal due in six weeks. Winning this project means a significant contract; losing it means budget cuts and possible layoffs. Kabir, promoted to team lead just three weeks ago over two other senior candidates, is leading the effort.

By Week 2, cracks are already forming. Riya, one of the most experienced analysts, has been visibly disengaged — missing two internal deadlines and delivering below her usual standard. Nobody has addressed it directly.

Meanwhile, Arjun has taken over most team discussions. He interrupts others mid-sentence, dismisses ideas he disagrees with, and has twice made sarcastic remarks about Priya's suggestions in front of the group. Priya has stopped speaking up entirely. Dev, frustrated on her behalf, has started arguing back at Arjun, and their disagreement over the final presentation has turned personal and is affecting the whole team's pace.

Kabir has privately confided in you that he overheard two senior members — including Arjun — saying management "got the promotion wrong." He is visibly shaken but hasn't addressed it.

It is now the morning of the final client presentation. With ten minutes to go, you notice a significant calculation error in the financial slide — the one Riya was responsible for. She is standing nearby, already anxious. Kabir has just been informed. The client is waiting in the next room.
